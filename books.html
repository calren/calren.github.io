Recent Book Adventures

Hover over the book covers for a short review!

Bill Clinton: My Life

Not gonna lie, I didn't finish this ginormous book. I read pretty closely in the first ~100 pages when Bill talked more about his personal life before he got into politics, but once his politics life started, I found it really hard to maintain my interest. Skimmed through the rest.

★★★☆☆

Lover's Dictionary

This was a super short book, and I finished it within one sitting at a bookstore. Loved the way the story progressed, the writer's style, and just the general concept of the whole book.

★★★★★

Secret

My first introduction to graphic novels, and I really liked the chiller reading pace. The concept of the novel was pretty interesting, but I was a little disappointed by the ending.

★★★☆☆

The Storied Life of AJ Filkry

Easily one of my favorite books of all time. It's a short book and I finished it within a couple hours, but loved every moment so much. The characters were loveable, I LOLed countless times, and the plot has unpredictable turns. The book itself is structured through short stories that AJ picked out, so I wish I read all the short stories before reading each chapter. Will save that for next time.

★★★★★

The Enchanted

Loved how the book was written and the emotional journeys the characters went through. Wish it was longer though.

★★★★☆

Paper Towns

My third John Green book completed. This one was just okay. Thought the main character was too unreasonly obsessed with the girl.

★★★☆☆

An Officer and a Spy

I love historical fiction books, and this one about the Dreyfus affair was great. The characters were interesting, and the plot was so intense I couldn't believe it was all true.

★★★★☆

The Husband's Secret

Seemed like one of those more trashy novels to read on a whim. Surprise twist ending was the highlight.

★★★☆☆

The Invention of Wings

Read it because Oprah approved it. It was more like a typical slavery novel though, and wasn't particularly memorable.

★★★★☆

The Orphan Train

This book was definitely full of depressing moments, as one can tell from the title itself. However, the story was told through the perspective of two different characters, and of course, their story entertwined in the end in one of those "aha!" moments. Also had one of those feel-good endings that's suited for everybody.

★★★★☆

The Pact

Really loved The Storyteller, so decided to chose another Jodi Pocult book to read. This one was sadly no where as good, and it felt really dragged on for the most part. About 1/3 through the book I just skimmed the rest to find out what happens in the end.

★★★☆☆

The Other Wes Moore

Per a DC resident's suggestion. Definitely one of the better books I've read recently. I think I mostly enjoyed it seeing how our lives can change so dramatically based on just a few key points and decisions in our everyday lives.

★★★★☆
